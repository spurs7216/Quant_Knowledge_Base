# Probability Theory The Logic of Science - Ch 05 Queer Uses for Probability Theory

## Role of the chapter

This chapter uses odd examples to force a practical question: how strongly do we really believe a claim, and how much evidence would be needed to move that belief materially?

## Core objects

- degrees of belief
- evidence thresholds
- odds and decibel-style belief scaling

## Selected technical spine

The chapter's durable lesson is methodological: priors should be inspectable in terms of the amount of evidence required to overturn them. That is more useful than pretending priors do not exist.

## Quant research relevance

This matters for hypothesis skepticism in alpha research. If a claimed edge would require implausibly strong evidence to overcome prior disbelief, the research process should say that explicitly.
