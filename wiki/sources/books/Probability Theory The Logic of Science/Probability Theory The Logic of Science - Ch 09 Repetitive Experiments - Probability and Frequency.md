# Probability Theory The Logic of Science - Ch 09 Repetitive Experiments - Probability and Frequency

## Ingest Scope and Depth

- Ingest stage: `selective_deepen`
- Pass 1: full chapter scan completed.
- Pass 2: selected the probability-versus-frequency distinction as the durable core.

## Role of the chapter

This chapter argues that probabilities are not identical to frequencies, even though frequencies can be modeled and predicted by probability theory.

## Core objects

- repetitive experiments
- exchangeability
- probability as logic
- observed frequency

## Selected technical spine

The durable claim is conceptual: frequencies are data-like empirical summaries, while probabilities are rational degrees of belief conditioned on information. One can use probability to estimate frequencies without collapsing the two notions.

## Quant research relevance

This matters in backtesting and empirical finance because estimated hit rates or event frequencies do not automatically exhaust the uncertainty of the problem.
