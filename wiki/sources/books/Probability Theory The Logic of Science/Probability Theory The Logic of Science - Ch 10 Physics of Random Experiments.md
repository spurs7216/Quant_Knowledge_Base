# Probability Theory The Logic of Science - Ch 10 Physics of Random Experiments

## Role of the chapter

Jaynes argues that so-called random experiments are still physical systems. Probability enters because of incomplete information about those systems, not because randomness is a magical ontological primitive.

## Core objects

- physical mechanism
- incomplete information
- symmetry assumptions
- exchangeability versus independence

## Selected technical spine

The main durable point is diagnostic: whenever someone calls an experiment random, ask what structural information is actually known, what symmetries are being used, and what is being left unspecified.

## Quant research relevance

This matters for market simulation, Monte Carlo design, and any setting where "randomness" is a compressed label for unknown mechanism plus modeling assumptions.
