# Probability Theory The Logic of Science - Ch 16 Orthodox Statistics - Historical Background

## Ingest Scope and Depth

- Ingest stage: `scan`
- Pass 1: chapter scan completed.
- Pass 2: no deeper theorem extraction, because this chapter is mainly historical framing for the critique developed in Chapter 17.

## Role of the chapter

This chapter sets up the institutional and conceptual background of orthodox statistics. Its purpose is not to add new probability rules but to explain how fixed-sample significance testing, confidence procedures, and anti-prior ideology became dominant.

## Core takeaways

- frequentist orthodoxy historically separated inference from direct probability statements about hypotheses
- that separation created pathologies around optional stopping, nuisance parameters, and model comparison
- the chapter is mainly a staging ground for the technical critique in the next chapter

## Why this matters later

Without the historical background, Jaynes' later attacks on orthodox procedures can sound merely rhetorical. This chapter explains why those procedures were adopted and why they remained entrenched despite logical weakness.

## Quant research relevance

The chapter matters mainly as intellectual context. It helps explain why modern empirical finance still inherits habits like p-value worship, mechanical significance testing, and discomfort with explicit priors.
