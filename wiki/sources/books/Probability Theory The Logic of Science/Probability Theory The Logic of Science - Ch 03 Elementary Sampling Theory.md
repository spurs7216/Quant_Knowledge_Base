# Probability Theory The Logic of Science - Ch 03 Elementary Sampling Theory

## Ingest Scope and Depth

- Ingest stage: `selective_deepen`
- Pass 1: full chapter scan completed.
- Pass 2: selected the Bernoulli urn logic and finite-sampling rules as the durable core.

## Role of the chapter

This chapter shows how far ordinary probability calculus already goes before any advanced asymptotics. The emphasis is finite combinatorial reasoning, not frequency mythology.

## Core objects

- mutually exclusive and exhaustive hypotheses
- principle of indifference
- sampling with and without replacement
- Bernoulli urn rule

## Selected technical spine

The reusable point is that standard sampling formulas follow from:

$$\Prob(AB \mid C)=\Prob(A \mid BC)\Prob(B \mid C)$$

plus finite symmetry assumptions. The chapter therefore treats sampling distributions as derived consequences of prior structure, not primitive axioms.

## Quant research relevance

This chapter is less important for direct quant modeling than later Jaynes chapters, but it matters as a reminder that finite-sample logic and explicit conditioning often beat asymptotic handwaving.

## What should be promoted out of this source note

- finite-sampling logic remains a useful support note for exact tests and exchangeability
