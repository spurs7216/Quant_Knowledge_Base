# Heard on the Street

## Summary

This interview-prep book is organized as a large question bank spanning quantitative logic, derivatives, financial economics, statistics, and non-technical questions. It is not a theory text, but it is useful as a stress-test source for whether the vault's knowledge can be explained under interview pressure rather than only in essay form.

## Chapter-by-Chapter Ingest

- `Chapter 1. Purely Quantitative and Logic Questions`: drills the arithmetic, probability, combinatorial, and puzzle-solving reflexes expected in quant interviews.
- `Chapter 2. Derivatives Questions`: focuses on options, fixed income, and pricing intuition under interview constraints.
- `Chapter 3. Other Financial Economics Questions`: broadens into market and finance intuition beyond narrow derivatives math.
- `Chapter 4. Statistics Questions`: tests statistical reasoning in compact problem form.
- `Chapter 5. Non-Quantitative Questions`: covers behavioral and communication components of the interview process.

## Key Claims

- Interview performance depends on speed, structured thinking, and explanation quality, not only raw knowledge.
- Technical breadth still matters; interviewers often mix puzzles, finance, statistics, and soft questions.
- Quant preparation should include verbalization and framing, not only silent problem solving.

## Methods and Data

- probability and logic problems
- derivative-pricing intuition
- finance and economics interview drills
- statistics interview questions
- non-technical interview preparation

## Why It Matters Here

This source is useful for a candidate-facing layer of the vault. It can help translate deep knowledge into explainable, interview-ready answers.

## Reflection

The value here is diagnostic rather than foundational. A vault can contain deep notes, but this kind of book exposes where that knowledge fails to compress into a clear live explanation.

## Caveats

- The book is a question bank rather than a coherent theory text.
- Interview style varies across firms, so this is best used as a practice surface rather than an oracle.
