# Arbitrage Theory in Continuous Time - Ch 16 Dividends

## Ingest Scope and Depth

- Ingest stage: `scan`
- Pass 1: chapter scan completed.
- Pass 2: no separate theorem deepen; the chapter mainly extends earlier pricing machinery to dividend-paying assets.

## Role of the chapter

This chapter explains how dividends alter the price process, carry, and valuation formulas without changing the basic arbitrage logic.

## Core mathematical objects

- discrete dividend jumps
- continuous dividend yield
- dividend-adjusted stock dynamics

## Structural map of the chapter

- discrete dividends
- continuous dividends by classical methods
- continuous dividends by martingale methods

## Theorem and derivation spine

The main durable point is operational:

- dividends change the underlying gain process and parity relations
- numeraire and martingale methods still work once the dividend structure is modeled correctly

## Failure modes and misuse points

- using non-dividend formulas in dividend-paying settings
- forgetting jump conditions at ex-dividend dates

## Quant research relevance

This chapter is support material for equity derivatives and carry-sensitive pricing.
