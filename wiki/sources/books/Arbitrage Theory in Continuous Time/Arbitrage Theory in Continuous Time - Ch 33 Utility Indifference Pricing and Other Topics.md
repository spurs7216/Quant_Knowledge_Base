## Role of the chapter

This chapter gives the most preference-based pricing rule in the book: price is whatever cash amount makes the investor indifferent between holding and not holding the claim.

## Core mathematical objects

- utility indifference price $p$
- value function with and without the claim
- marginal indifference price as local derivative price

## Structural map of the chapter

- define global indifference pricing
- linearize to marginal indifference pricing
- connect the price rule back to hedging

## Theorem and derivation spine

The durable point is conceptual:

- when markets are incomplete, valuation can depend on preferences and endowments rather than only on replication or one selected martingale measure

## Failure modes and misuse points

- treating an indifference price as market-wide rather than investor-specific
- forgetting that inventory and endowment matter

## Quant research relevance

This chapter is important for incomplete-market derivatives, xVA-style thinking, and inventory-sensitive valuation.
