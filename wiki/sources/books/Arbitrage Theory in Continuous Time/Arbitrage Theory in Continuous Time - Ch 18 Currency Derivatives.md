# Arbitrage Theory in Continuous Time - Ch 18 Currency Derivatives

## Ingest Scope and Depth

- Ingest stage: `scan`
- Pass 1: chapter scan completed.
- Pass 2: no full theorem deepen; this chapter mainly applies numeraire logic to FX markets.

## Role of the chapter

This chapter shows that domestic and foreign money accounts are natural numeraires and that FX derivatives are one of the cleanest places to see numeraire choice in action.

## Core mathematical objects

- domestic and foreign numeraires
- FX spot
- foreign equity viewed from domestic currency
- Siegel paradox

## Structural map of the chapter

- pure currency contracts
- martingale approach
- domestic versus foreign equity markets
- extended Black-Scholes model

## Theorem and derivation spine

The durable point is conceptual:

- FX pricing is fundamentally a change-of-numeraire problem
- domestic and foreign discount curves matter symmetrically but under different benchmarks

## Failure modes and misuse points

- pricing FX claims as if there were only one cash account
- ignoring which currency defines the payoff and hedge benchmark

## Quant research relevance

This chapter is useful as a carry and numeraire reference, but the core machinery is already promoted elsewhere.
