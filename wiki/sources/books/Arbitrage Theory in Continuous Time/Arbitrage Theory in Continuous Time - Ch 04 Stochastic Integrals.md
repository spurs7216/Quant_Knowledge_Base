## Role of the chapter

This chapter turns Brownian motion into a usable calculus. It is the technical gateway from continuous-time noise to self-financing trading and pricing.

## Core mathematical objects

- Wiener process $W$
- stochastic integral
  $$
  X_t=\int_0^t g_u\,dW_u
  $$
- Ito isometry
  $$
  \E\bracket{\paren{\int_s^t g_u\,dW_u}^2}=\E\int_s^t g_u^2\,du
  $$
- stochastic differential
  $$
  dX_t=\mu_t\,dt+\sigma_t\,dW_t
  $$

## Structural map of the chapter

- define the Wiener process and emphasize path roughness
- define stochastic integrals first for simple integrands and then in $L^2$
- identify the martingale class generated by zero-drift diffusions
- derive the Ito formula and its multidimensional extension

## Theorem and derivation spine

### Wiener paths are rough enough to break ordinary calculus

The chapter states that Wiener trajectories are almost surely nowhere differentiable and of locally infinite variation. That is why ordinary chain rules fail.

### Zero drift is the martingale signature

The chapter proves the practical criterion:
$$
dX_t=g_t\,dW_t
$$
if and only if $X$ is a martingale, subject to the required integrability.

That criterion later becomes the hidden logic behind pricing under martingale measures.

### Ito's formula adds the quadratic-variation term

For a diffusion
$$
dX_t=\mu_t\,dt+\sigma_t\,dW_t,
$$
and a smooth function $F(t,x)$, the chapter's central result is
$$
dF(t,X_t)=\paren{F_t+\mu_t F_x+\frac{1}{2}\sigma_t^2 F_{xx}}dt+\sigma_t F_x\,dW_t.
$$

This is the formula that later creates pricing PDEs, term-structure equations, HJB equations, and free-boundary systems.

## Failure modes and misuse points

- treating $dW_t/dt$ as an ordinary process
- forgetting that Ito calculus is driven by quadratic variation
- using deterministic chain rules inside stochastic models
