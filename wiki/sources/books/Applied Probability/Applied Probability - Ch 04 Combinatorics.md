## What the chapter is really doing

### 1. It reminds the reader that many probability problems are counting problems first

Before asymptotics or transforms enter, many exact discrete laws come from careful enumeration.

### 2. Inclusion-exclusion stays relevant far beyond toy problems

The chapter uses it in settings that later echo rare-event counting and occupancy logic.

### 3. Special counting sequences matter when structure is recursive

Catalan and Stirling numbers are not main vault priorities right now, but they mark the kinds of recursive combinatorial structure that often hide inside branching and path-counting problems.
