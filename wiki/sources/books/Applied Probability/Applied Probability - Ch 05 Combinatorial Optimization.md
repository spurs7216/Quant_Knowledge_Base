## What the chapter is really doing

### 1. It connects probability to algorithmic performance

Quick sort and coding examples show that random structure often determines expected cost and typical behavior more directly than worst-case analysis does.

### 2. It treats combinatorial optimization as a probability-adjacent subject

The chapter is a reminder that counting, symmetry, and expectation arguments often explain why discrete algorithms behave as they do.

### 3. It prepares later simulated-annealing intuition

The graph-coloring and optimization examples pair naturally with the Markov-chain chapter's treatment of simulated annealing.
