# A Practical Guide to Quantitative Finance Interviews

## Summary

Xinfeng Zhou's interview-prep book is organized as a broad seven-part curriculum: interview principles, brainteasers, calculus and linear algebra, probability, stochastic processes and stochastic calculus, finance, and algorithms / numerical methods. It is closer to a candidate roadmap than a pure question bank and therefore useful as a coverage map for what a quant candidate is expected to explain on demand.

## Chapter-by-Chapter Ingest

- `Chapter 1. General Principles`: frames interview preparation as a skill-building and communication problem, not only a knowledge problem.
- `Chapter 2. Brain Teasers`: trains structured reasoning under unusual constraints and incomplete information.
- `Chapter 3. Calculus and Linear Algebra`: covers the mathematical core that underpins modeling, optimization, and derivatives work.
- `Chapter 4. Probability Theory`: drills the probability toolkit expected in quant interviews.
- `Chapter 5. Stochastic Process and Stochastic Calculus`: moves from basic probability into dynamic uncertainty and continuous-time reasoning.
- `Chapter 6. Finance`: tests whether mathematics can be connected to actual financial instruments and intuition.
- `Chapter 7. Algorithms and Numerical Methods`: treats implementation and computational reasoning as part of quant competence.

## Key Claims

- Quant interviews test breadth across math, probability, finance, and algorithms.
- Technical knowledge must be paired with structured communication and problem-solving habits.
- Stochastic calculus and numerical methods are interview topics, not just desk-side specialist material.
